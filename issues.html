Troubleshoot Like a Pro

Building a $1 website is thrilling, but hiccups can happen. We faced issues like missing emails and DNS errors while creating $1 Web Revolution. This page shares our fixes to keep your project smooth, plus tips to use Grok for debugging and customization. Let’s keep your site on track!

Even with Grok’s AI, snags can pop up. Below, we cover common issues, step-by-step fixes, and how to leverage Grok to troubleshoot or customize your site. Start smart with IONOS to avoid headaches!

1. IONOS Account Mix-Ups

Using multiple IONOS accounts caused our domain (webfor1.com) to disappear from the dashboard, stalling setup.

Fix: Sign up with one email at IONOS. If your domain isn’t in your dashboard, contact support (1-844-494-9067 or support@ionos.com) with your Customer ID and Contract Number. They’ll link it to your account.

Pro Tip: Use a single, reliable email. Ask Grok to draft a support email (e.g., “Write an email to IONOS about my missing domain”).

2. Missing ICANN Verification Email

The ICANN email verifies domain ownership, but it didn’t arrive for us, delaying activation.

Fix: Check spam/junk folders. If missing, go to IONOS > Domains > Privacy & Contact Details, update your email, and request a resend. Contact support if it persists.

Pro Tip: Set up email filters for IONOS emails. Ask Grok to review your setup (e.g., “Did I miss a step in IONOS?”).

3. DNS Misconfiguration

Incorrect DNS settings caused “server not found” errors when linking our domain to Netlify.

Fix: In IONOS > Domains > DNS, add:

  • CNAME: Host = www, Value = webfor1.netlify.app, TTL = 3600.
  • A record: Host = @, Value = 75.2.60.5, TTL = 3600.

Delete conflicting A/AAAA records. Wait 1-2 hours and check at dnschecker.org.

Pro Tip: Ask Grok to verify DNS (e.g., “Check my CNAME for www.webfor1.com”). Test in a private tab to confirm.

4. Site Display Issues

Images or layouts (like our hero image) looked off due to coding errors or missing files.

Fix: Ensure files (index.html, /images/hero.jpg) are in the GitHub repo root or images folder. Check Netlify’s Deploys tab for errors. Ask Grok to fix issues (e.g., “My image is cropped—help!”).

Pro Tip: Clear browser cache or test in a private tab. Ask Grok for style tweaks (e.g., “Make images full-width without cropping”).

5. Images Not Loading

Images may fail to load due to incorrect paths or large file sizes slowing down your site.

Fix: Verify image paths in your HTML (e.g., /images/hero.jpg). Ensure images are in the images folder in your GitHub repo. Use JPGs under 1MB—resize in Canva if needed. Test in a private tab.

Pro Tip: Ask Grok to check image paths (e.g., “Why isn’t my hero.jpg loading?”). Compress images at tinypng.com for faster load times.

6. Mobile Responsiveness Issues

Your site may look off on mobile (e.g., iPhone) due to missing responsive design settings.

Fix: Ensure your HTML includes <meta name="viewport" content="width=device-width, initial-scale=1.0">. Use Tailwind’s responsive classes (e.g., md:text-xl). Ask Grok to adjust CSS (e.g., “Fix my navbar for mobile”). Test on your iPhone in Safari.

Pro Tip: Preview your site in Safari’s Developer Tools (Responsive Design Mode) or ask Grok to optimize (e.g., “Make my site mobile-friendly”).
